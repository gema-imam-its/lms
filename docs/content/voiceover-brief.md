# Voice-Over Brief — Mascot Narration

> **Draft for internal (PM) review.** A **Bahasa Indonesia** version will be prepared before
> sending to the voice actor. Depends on the guru's content being **locked** first (see §7).

## 1. The project &amp; who's listening

An LMS that teaches sholat to **SLB students** (intellectual disability, and some deaf/hard-of-
hearing). You are the **voice of the mascot** — a friendly on-screen guide.

> **The one rule that shapes everything:** the voice **supports** what's on screen; it never
> carries essential information alone, because some children can't hear it. So your lines are
> warm reinforcement and encouragement — the app is fully understandable with the sound off.

## 2. Character &amp; tone

The mascot is a **kind, patient, cheerful friend** — like a gentle teacher a child adores.
Not a hyperactive cartoon, never stern.

- **Simple Bahasa Indonesia**, everyday words a young child knows.
- **Slow and clear**, with small pauses. Give the child time.
- **Warm and encouraging — especially on mistakes. Never disappointed or scolding.**
- **Consistent** character and energy across every single line.

## 3. What gets recorded (two groups)

- **A. System lines** — the mascot's stock phrases, reused across *every* lesson. **Record these
  first**; they're usable immediately (§4).
- **B. Per-slide guide lines** — only on slides the guru marked "Suara: ya". These come from the
  curriculum intake; the PM sends you the **final** list with exact file IDs (§5).

## 6. Recording &amp; delivery specs

- Language: **Bahasa Indonesia**.
- **One audio file per line.**
- Format: **WAV 48 kHz, mono** (or MP3 320 kbps if WAV isn't possible).
- **Quiet room**, no background music or noise, consistent mic distance.
- **File naming is critical** — it's how we attach each clip to the right slide:
  - System lines → `sys-<name>.wav` (exactly as in §4).
  - Slide lines → `m<module>-s<slide>.wav` (PM gives you the exact IDs).
- Deliver to: _[PM to fill — shared Drive folder / link]_.

## 7. Process &amp; order (so nothing gets re-recorded)

1. **Guru finalizes** the lesson content (curriculum intake).
2. **PM extracts &amp; locks** the script list, sends you the §5 table.
3. You record **system lines first** (they never change), then the per-slide lines.
4. Deliver named files → we wire them into the slides.

> ⚠️ **Please don't start the per-slide lines until the script list is marked FINAL.** Teacher
> wording can still change; the system lines in §4 are safe to record now.
